title: "WhatsApp Stock Selection Scams"
description: "Fraudsters using fake institutional groups and customer support names to push retail traders into bogus investment portfolios."
category: "WhatsApp Scams"
whatIsIt: "WhatsApp Scams involve organized gangs setting up groups under names of famous global institutions (e.g., Goldman Sachs, BlackRock India). They share market news daily and introduce fake 'institutional accounts' or proprietary apps to extract deposits."
howItWorks:
  - "Infiltration: Users are added to random WhatsApp groups containing 50-200 members."
  - "Social Proof: Co-conspirators inside the group post messages praising the 'assistant' or 'mentor'."
  - "App Redirection: Victims are urged to download an off-store application to trade with 'institutional leverage'."
  - "The Block: When the user requests a withdrawal, they are asked for 'tax fees' before being blocked."
warningSigns:
  - "Being added to an investment group without your prior consent"
  - "Unrealistic claims of accessing special institutional allocations or IPO blocks"
  - "Demands to install apps via APK files or links instead of official app stores"
  - "Customer service requiring money deposits to random personal accounts for loading wallets"
preventionTips:
  - "Enable privacy settings in WhatsApp to prevent unknown contacts from adding you to groups."
  - "Never install trading applications that are not verified on Google Play Store or Apple App Store."
  - "Verify bank details; legitimate brokerages use institutional ESCROW accounts, never personal bank transfers."
realExamples:
  - "An investor was added to a group named 'Morgan Stanley Elite Club'. He downloaded a custom app via a link and deposited ₹5,00,000. The app showed a balance of ₹15,00,000 from fake trades, but when he tried to withdraw, they asked for a ₹1.5L withdrawal tax and blocked him."
