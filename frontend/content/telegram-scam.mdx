---
title: "Telegram Premium Group Scams"
description: "Fraudulent channels promising insider stock tips and crypto pumps for a VIP subscription fee."
category: "Telegram Scams"
whatIsIt: "Telegram Premium Group scams involve fraudsters setting up channels posing as elite SEBI-registered advisory firms or high-profile traders. They offer 'VIP' or 'Premium' memberships for a fee, promising insider information and high-probability trading signals."
howItWorks:
  - "The Invitation: Scammers harvest contact numbers or add users randomly to public groups."
  - "The Bait: They post screenshots of successful trades (often photoshopped) and fake testimonies."
  - "The Upsell: To access the 'jackpot' trades, they demand payment for a VIP subscription."
  - "The Ghosting: Once the fee is paid, the tips result in losses, and the admin deletes the chat."
warningSigns:
  - "Promises of guaranteed daily or weekly returns (e.g., 5% daily)"
  - "Admins demanding payments in cryptocurrency or personal bank accounts"
  - "High-pressure tactics like 'Only 3 slots left for today'"
  - "Group chat is locked, and only admins can post messages"
preventionTips:
  - "Always check if the advisor is SEBI-registered on the official SEBI portal."
  - "Never share payments with personal bank accounts or virtual IDs."
  - "Treat all screenshots of high-earning trades as manipulated until verified."
realExamples:
  - "An investor joined a channel called 'HNI Advisor Wealth' and paid ₹25,000 for a subscription. He was then advised to buy an illiquid penny stock, which crashed 40% the next day while the group admins blocked him."

  - question: "What should you do before transferring advisory fees?"
    options:

      - "Send cryptocurrency to save on tax"
    answer: "Verify the registration credentials on SEBI's official site"
