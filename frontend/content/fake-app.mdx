whatIsIt: "Fake Trading Applications are malicious trading applications downloaded via links rather than app stores. These apps mimic authentic trading platforms (like MetaTrader, Zerodha, or foreign brokers) but connect to a server controlled by scammers. All trades and balances shown are completely fabricated."
howItWorks:
  - "Redirection: Scammers guide you to download their specific app via an external link (APK/IPA file)."
  - "Demo Manipulation: The app creates an account loaded with fake profits to build confidence."
  - "Deposit Pressure: You are urged to deposit larger sums (lakhs) to maximize your returns."
  - "The Lockout: If you try to withdraw, the app shows 'withdrawal review failed' or demands custom custom tax fees."
warningSigns:
  - "App is not hosted on Google Play Store or iOS App Store"
  - "App requires you to disable security features on your phone to install (e.g. 'Install from Unknown Sources')"
  - "Deposits are made to varying private bank accounts rather than a unified brokerage account name"
  - "App interface contains typos, broken buttons, or poor navigation"
preventionTips:
  - "Only trade through SEBI-registered brokers listed on the NSE and BSE websites."
  - "Never bypass your phone's security settings to install third-party apps."
  - "Search for reviews and ratings on Google Play or Trustpilot before utilizing any broker."
realExamples:
  - "A user installed a trading app called 'TradeMax Global' via a WhatsApp link. The app showed he made ₹2,00,000 in two days on BTC. When he attempted to withdraw ₹50,000, the system locked his account, and support demanded a ₹60,000 'activation fee' to unlock it."
