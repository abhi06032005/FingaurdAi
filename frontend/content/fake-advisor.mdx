---
title: "Fake Advisory Scams"
description: "Unregistered individuals posing as SEBI stock advisors charging hefty fees for poor or non-existent stock selections."
category: "Fake Advisors"
whatIsIt: "Fake Advisory Scams occur when unregistered analysts pose as financial advisors on social media, websites, or YouTube. They claim high accuracy on trades and ask you to buy their premium advisory plans or invest your capital directly with them."
howItWorks:
  - "The Pitch: Scammers post flashy videos showing luxurious lifestyles and claiming it's all from their trading tips."
  - "The Selection: They offer a 'free trial' or 'guaranteed' stock recommendations for a fee."
  - "Profit Sharing: Some propose 'profit sharing' schemes where they take 50% of profits but leave you with 100% of losses."
  - "Capital Custody: In worst cases, they ask you to deposit money in their account so they can trade on your behalf."
warningSigns:
  - "Lack of a SEBI registration number (or using a photoshopped/stolen SEBI certificate)"
  - "Asking to manage your trading account credentials (username/password sharing)"
  - "Guarantees of profit (SEBI rules explicitly forbid advisors from guaranteeing returns)"
  - "Demands for profit sharing without any legal contract"
preventionTips:
  - "Verify all advisor names and registration numbers on the official SEBI intermediary lookup page."
  - "Never share your trading terminal login password, PIN, or OTPs with anyone."
  - "Be skeptical of anyone showing cash stacks or luxury cars as proof of trading accuracy."
realExamples:
  - "A user paid ₹40,000 to a self-proclaimed 'options king' on Instagram. He was told to buy call options that expired worthless. When the user complained, the advisor accused him of 'wrong execution' and tried to sell him a recovery course for ₹20,000."
