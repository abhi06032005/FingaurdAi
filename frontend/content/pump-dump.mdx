description: "Artificially inflating a stock's price through false hype before selling off, leaving retail investors stranded."
category: "Pump and Dump"
whatIsIt: "Pump and Dump schemes occur when organizers buy massive positions in low-volume, illiquid stocks, then hype them up on social media (Twitter, Telegram, YouTube) to trigger buying panic. Once the price jumps, they sell (dump) their shares, causing the price to crash."
howItWorks:
  - "Accumulation: Scammers quietly accumulate a large position in a micro-cap or penny stock."
  - "The Hype: They blast fake news, target price alerts, and screenshots to dozens of channels."
  - "The Peak: Excited retail investors rush in, driving the stock price up rapidly."
  - "The Dump: Scammers sell off all their holdings, causing the stock to lock in lower circuits."
warningSigns:
  - "Unsolicited stock tips received via SMS, WhatsApp, or Telegram groups"
  - "Penny stocks showing volume spikes without any concrete regulatory disclosures"
  - "Price targets showing unrealistic upside (e.g. 500% in 1 month)"
  - "Tips urging you to buy immediately because of 'insider info'"
preventionTips:
  - "Never buy micro-cap stocks based solely on social media or SMS tips."
  - "Check company financials for actual revenues, cash flow, and promoter pledges."
  - "Avoid stocks that hit consecutive upper circuits without any underlying company changes."
realExamples:
  - "A penny stock of an agricultural firm was hyped on WhatsApp as 'the next multibagger'. It hit upper circuits for 10 days, growing from ₹5 to ₹35. On day 11, the volume vanished as the creators dumped, locking the stock in lower circuits back to ₹3."
