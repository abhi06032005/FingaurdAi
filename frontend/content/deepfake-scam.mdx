title: "Deepfake Influencer Scams"
description: "AI-generated videos of prominent financial figures or celebrities endorsing fraudulent trading algorithms or courses."
category: "Deepfake Scams"
whatIsIt: "Deepfake Influencer Scams leverage generative AI to create realistic video and audio clones of respected financial figures (e.g. Mukesh Ambani, Nithin Kamath, or Bollywood stars) promoting automated trading systems, courses, or wealth clubs that are actually scams."
howItWorks:
  - "The Video: Scammers edit short clips of famous leaders using AI tools to alter their voice and lip-sync."
  - "The Ad: They run these videos as sponsored advertisements on Instagram, YouTube, and Facebook."
  - "The Registration: Clicking the ad redirects you to a page asking for your contact information."
  - "The Pitch: A fake agent calls you claiming to represent the celebrity's exclusive investment fund."
warningSigns:
  - "Celebrity endorsing high-return trading software or automated bots"
  - "Video shows unnatural lip movements, voice lag, or blurry facial borders"
  - "The landing page URL is mismatching and uses generic domain names (e.g. wealth-club-india.xyz)"
  - "Endorsements of trading programs that claim 'No previous trading experience needed'"
preventionTips:
  - "Verify celebrity quotes on their official verified social media pages (blue tick accounts)."
  - "Be extremely skeptical of sponsored ads claiming guaranteed returns endorsed by HNIs."
  - "Check the video quality carefully: look for facial glitches, voice tone consistency, and audio-video synchronization."
realExamples:
  - "An ad on Facebook featured an AI-cloned video of Nithin Kamath (CEO of Zerodha) endorsing an 'AI Auto Trading Robot' that makes ₹25,000 per day. Investors who registered were contacted by scammers posing as account managers and lost their deposits."
